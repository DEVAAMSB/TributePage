Guido van Rossum

1956 - Present

Dutch Programmer, Python Creator

Guido van Rossum is a Dutch programmer and the creator of the Python programming language. He was born on January 31, 1956, in Haarlem, Netherlands. Guido started working on Python in the late 1980s and released the first version of the language in 1991.

Achievements

  • Guido van Rossum received the Free Software Foundation (FSF) Award for the Advancement of Free Software in 2001.
  • In 2005, he was appointed as a fellow of the Python Software Foundation.
  • Guido worked at Google from 2005 to 2012, where he continued to lead the development of Python.
  • He has been recognized as one of the most influential people in the world of technology.

Career

Throughout his career, Guido van Rossum has made significant contributions to the field of programming languages. He has worked on various projects and held positions at different organizations. Some of his notable career highlights include:


  • Working at the Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica (CWI) in the Netherlands.
